# The 100 Greatest Travel Books of All Time
## Final Category Structure

---

## I. First Routes and Foundational Journeys
**Travel as primary discovery and route-making**

Early, formative journeys where travel itself is revelation and structure.

1. The Travels — Marco Polo (1300)
2. The Rihla — Ibn Battuta (1355)
3. The First Voyage Around the World — Antonio Pigafetta (1525)
4. A Journey to the Western Islands of Scotland — Samuel Johnson (1775)
5. Personal Narrative — Alexander von Humboldt (1814)
6. The Voyage of the Beagle — Charles Darwin (1839)
7. Incidents of Travel in Yucatán — John Lloyd Stephens (1843)
8. The Oregon Trail — Francis Parkman (1849)
9. Travels in West Africa — Mary Kingsley (1897)
10. Sailing Alone Around the World — Joshua Slocum (1900)
11. The Blue Nile — Alan Moorehead (1962)

## II. Exploration at the Edge
**Physical extremity, endurance, and survival**

Land, climate, and distance dominate intention. Place presses back through hostility.

1. South — Ernest Shackleton (1919)
2. The Worst Journey in the World — Apsley Cherry-Garrard (1922)
3. Libyan Sands: Travel in a Dead World — Ralph Bagnold (1935)
4. The Cruel Way — Ella Maillart (1947)
5. Arabian Sands — Wilfred Thesiger (1959)
6. Coming into the Country — John McPhee (1977)
7. The Snow Leopard — Peter Matthiessen (1978)
8. Tracks — Robyn Davidson (1980)
9. Arctic Dreams — Barry Lopez (1986)
10. Maiden Voyage — Tania Aebi (1989)
11. Into the Wild — Jon Krakauer (1996)
12. The Cruelest Journey: 600 Miles to Timbuktu — Kira Salak (2004)

## III. Slow Routes and Long Ways Through
**Travel shaped by patience, rhythm, and continuity**

Walking, riding, sailing, lingering. Time and repetition deepen attention.

1. Travels with a Donkey in the Cévennes — Robert Louis Stevenson (1879)
2. The Path to Rome — Hilaire Belloc (1902)
3. The Man Who Walked Through Time — Colin Fletcher (1968)
4. As I Walked Out One Midsummer Morning — Laurie Lee (1969)
5. A Time of Gifts — Patrick Leigh Fermor (1977)

9. The Old Ways — Robert Macfarlane (2012)
10. Walking with Abel: Journeys with the Nomads of the African Savannah — Anna Badkhen (2015)
11. The Salt Path — Raynor Winn (2018)

2. A Ride to Khiva — Fred Burnaby (1876)
3. Unbeaten Tracks in Japan — Isabella Bird (1880)
4. Travels in Arabia Deserta — Charles Montagu Doughty (1888)

8. The Road to Oxiana — Robert Byron (1937)
9. In an Antique Land — Amitav Ghosh (1992)
10. From the Holy Mountain — William Dalrymple (1997)
11. An Unexpected Light: Travels in Afghanistan — Jason Elliott (1999)

13. The Great Hedge of India — Roy Moxham (2001)

## V. Humbled by the World
**Travel shaped by humor, ordinariness, or amateur limitations**

The world teaches through awkwardness. The traveler's own nature becomes the constraint.

1. A Short Walk in the Hindu Kush — Eric Newby (1958)
2. From Heaven Lake: Travels Through Sinkiang and Tibet — Vikram Seth (1983)
3. The Great American Bus Ride — Bill Adler (1986)
4. Video Night in Kathmandu: And Other Reports from the Not-So-Far East — Pico Iyer (1988)
5. A Walk in the Woods: Rediscovering America on the Appalachian Trail — Bill Bryson (1998)
6. Dark Continent My Black Arse — Sihle Khumalo (2007)
7. Turn Right at Machu Picchu: Rediscovering the Lost City One Step at a Time — Mark Adams (2011)
