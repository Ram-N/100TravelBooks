# XX — The Cruel Way

### *Ella Maillart*

**"We were two women alone, yet we were never really alone. We carried our own ghosts with us."** — *Ella Maillart*

---

### **[ The Essentials ]**

* **Genre:** Travel Memoir
* **Sub-Genre:** Psychological Adventure
* **Keywords:** *Afghanistan, 1939, Morphine Addiction, Ford, Kabul, Friendship*
* **Traveler Profile:** **The Healer.** You believe that a change of scenery can cure a broken soul.

---

### **[ The Author: Ella Maillart (1903–1997) ]**

Ella Maillart was a Swiss legend. She was a sailor and an Olympic skier and a fearless explorer. She had already crossed Central Asia on her own. She was tough and practical and she lived for the open road. But this journey was different. She wasn't just traveling for herself this time. She was trying to save her friend. She was a woman of deep empathy and a sharp eye for the shifting politics of a world about to go to war.

### **[ The Book: *The Cruel Way* ]**

This is a beautiful and heartbreaking story. In 1939, just as Europe was about to explode, Maillart set off from Geneva in a Ford. Her companion was Annemarie Schwarzenbach. In the book she is called "Christina." Christina was a brilliant writer and a wealthy socialite. She was also a severe morphine addict. Maillart thought that the high air of the Afghan mountains and the simple life of the road would help Christina get clean.

The writing is direct and incredibly powerful. Maillart uses small paragraphs to describe the dust of the Iranian deserts and the blue tiles of Herat. She has a dramatic flair for the tension between the two women. She is the steady one. She fixes the car and talks to the locals. Christina is the fragile one. She is a ghost haunting the passenger seat.

The "cruel way" refers to the landscape but also to the struggle of addiction. Maillart watches her friend slip away even as they pass through some of the most beautiful places on earth. She writes about the nomads and the ancient ruins with colorful detail. She makes you feel the heat of the day and the freezing cold of the mountain nights.

This is not a happy book. It is a record of a failed rescue mission. It shows that you can travel to the ends of the earth but you can never truly escape yourself. It is a masterpiece of travel writing that is as much about the human heart as it is about the map.

---

### **[ Destinations Explored ]**

* **The Balkans:** The first leg of the trip where the shadows of the coming war were already visible.
* **Turkey:** A land of high plateaus and sudden hospitality.
* **Herat:** The ancient Afghan city where the minarets reach for the sky.
* **Kabul:** The final destination where the journey—and the hope for Christina—finally reached its end.

---

### **[ Further Reading ]**

**By Maillart:**

* ***Forbidden Journey***: Her epic account of traveling from Peking to Kashmir with Peter Fleming.

**In a Similar Vein:**

* ***All the Roads Are Open*** by Annemarie Schwarzenbach: The same journey told from the perspective of the "ghost" herself.
* ***The Road to Oxiana*** by Robert Byron: A classic of 1930s travel that covers much of the same ground in Persia and Afghanistan.

### **[ The Perfect Pairing ]**
