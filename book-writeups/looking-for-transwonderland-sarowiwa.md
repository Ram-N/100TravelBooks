# XX — Looking for Transwonderland

### *Travels in Nigeria*

**"Nigeria is a country where the improbable happens on a daily basis."** — *Noo Saro-Wiwa*

---

### **[ The Essentials ]**

* **Genre:** Travel Memoir
* **Sub-Genre:** Return to Homeland / African Diaspora
* **Keywords:** *Nigeria, Lagos, Military Dictatorship, Family, Return, Chaos, Transwonderland*
* **Traveler Profile:** **The Brave Returning Soul.** You want to understand a place that is both your home and a complete stranger to you.

---

### **[ The Author: Noo Saro-Wiwa (1976–Present) ]**

Noo Saro-Wiwa was born in Nigeria but raised in England. She is the daughter of Ken Saro-Wiwa, the famous activist who was executed by the Nigerian military regime in 1995. For years, Nigeria was a place of grief and fear for her. She saw it as a "monstrous" country that took her father away. But eventually, she felt the pull to go back. She is a witty, observant, and deeply honest writer. She doesn't sugarcoat the problems of her homeland, but she also looks for the life and the humor beneath the surface.

### **[ The Book: *Looking for Transwonderland* ]**

The title comes from a crumbling, abandoned amusement park in Nigeria. It is a perfect metaphor for a country that has grand dreams but often struggles with the reality of daily life. Noo Saro-Wiwa travels from the madness of Lagos to the ancient cities of the north and the tropical forests of the south. She is looking for the Nigeria that exists beyond the headlines of corruption and oil.

The writing is punchy and very funny. She describes the "go-slows" (traffic jams) of Lagos and the "area boys" who demand money for everything. She talks about the bizarre "Nollywood" film industry and the intensity of Nigerian religious life. She has a dramatic flair for capturing the sheer noise and energy of the streets. She uses small paragraphs to keep the pace fast, mirroring the frantic energy of the country itself.

She deals with her family history with a quiet and moving dignity. She visits the places her father loved and the places where he suffered. She tries to reconcile her British upbringing with her Nigerian roots. She finds beauty in unexpected places, like the ancient walls of Kano or the hills of Sukur.

This is a story about a woman making peace with her past. She shows us a Nigeria that is frustrating, chaotic, and sometimes dangerous, but also incredibly resilient and full of spirit. She captures the colorful details of the markets and the sharp wit of the people she meets. It is a refreshingly honest look at a country that most people only see through a narrow lens.

### **[ Destinations Explored ]**

* **Lagos:** The "big smoke." A sprawling, high-octane city where everything is for sale and nothing is easy.
* **Kano:** A dusty, ancient trading city in the north with a rich history and a very different pace of life.
* **The Niger Delta:** Her ancestral home, a place of immense natural beauty and tragic environmental destruction.
* **Transwonderland:** The decaying theme park that serves as a haunting reminder of what might have been.

### **[ Further Reading ]**

* ***Black Ghosts***: Her later work exploring the lives of African immigrants in China.

**In a Similar Vein:**

* ***Every Day is for the Thief*** by Teju Cole: A novella that captures the same sense of a restless return to Lagos.
