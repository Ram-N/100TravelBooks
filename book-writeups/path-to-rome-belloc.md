# XX — The Path to Rome

### *Hilaire Belloc*

**"I will start from the place where I served in arms... and I will walk straight to Rome."** — *Hilaire Belloc*

---

### **[ The Essentials ]**

* **Genre:** Travel Memoir / Philosophical Essay
* **Sub-Genre:** Catholic Pilgrimage
* **Keywords:** *The Alps, 1901, Hiking, Catholicism, Wine, Sketching, Europe*
* **Traveler Profile:** **The High-Spirited Wanderer.** You believe that a good walk requires three things: a stout stick, a flask of wine, and a complete disregard for the advice of others.

---

### **[ The Author: Hilaire Belloc (1870–1953) ]**

Hilaire Belloc was a giant of English letters—a poet, historian, politician, and polemicist. Born in France but raised in England, he was a man of immense physical and intellectual energy. He was a fierce defender of the Catholic faith and a lover of European tradition. He didn't just write books; he roared them onto the page. He was a man who believed in the "Old World"—the world of peasant farms, ancient inns, and local spirits. In 1901, at the age of thirty, he decided to perform a "vow" he had made to himself: to walk from Toul in France to Rome, traveling in a straight line, sleeping rough, and arriving in time for High Mass at St. Peter's.

### **[ The Book: *The Path to Rome* ]**

This is not a guidebook; it is a celebration of the act of living. Belloc sets out from the garrison town of Toul, where he had served his stint in the French artillery. He carries little more than a sketch-block and a fierce determination to cross the Alps and the Apennines on foot.

The writing is conversational, eccentric, and full of dramatic flair. Belloc constantly interrupts his own narrative to argue with a fictional "Lector," to launch into a poem, or to describe a particular bottle of wine in painstaking detail. He uses small paragraphs to capture the sudden, breathtaking beauty of a mountain sunrise or the grim exhaustion of a rainy night in a hayloft.

He has a colorful way of describing the people he meets—Italian peasants, Swiss innkeepers, and fellow travelers. He doesn't just observe the landscape; he interacts with it like a long-lost friend. He writes about the "oneness" of Europe, arguing that the true soul of the continent is found on its footpaths, not in its parliaments. It is a book filled with sketches (drawn by Belloc himself) that capture the crumbling stone walls and soaring peaks of his journey. It is a story of a man who walks himself into a state of spiritual and physical grace.

### **[ Destinations Explored ]**

* **Toul:** The starting point in the Lorraine region of France, the "cradle" of his journey.
* **The Jura Mountains:** Where he first tests his endurance against the rising elevation.
* **The Gries Pass:** The high, snowy crossing into Italy that nearly broke his spirit and his legs.
* **The Tuscan Hills:** Where the air turns warm and the landscape becomes a Renaissance painting.
* **Rome:** The Eternal City, the goal of his vow, and the end of the long, dusty road.

---

### **[ Further Reading ]**

**By Belloc:**

* ***The Four Men***: A "fictionalized" travelogue of a walk across the county of Sussex.
* ***The Cruise of the 'Nona'***: His meditations on the sea and life while sailing around the coast of Britain.

**In a Similar Vein:**

* ***As I Walked Out One Midsummer Morning*** by Laurie Lee: For another classic account of a young man walking across a changing Europe.
* ***A Time of Gifts*** by Patrick Leigh Fermor: The ultimate spiritual successor to Belloc—a young man walking from Holland to Constantinople.

### **[ The Signature Line ]**

> "But for the Lord's sake, do not tell me that I was right in my geography; for I have had enough of the road, and I want to hear no more of it."

### **[ The Perfect Pairing ]**

* **The Drink:** A large carafe of local red wine, served in a thick glass at a roadside tavern.
* **The Soundtrack:** The steady rhythm of boots on gravel and the sound of a man singing loudly (and poorly) to keep the shadows away.
* **The Setting:** A high mountain ridge at dawn, with the clouds below you and the entire world spread out like a map.
