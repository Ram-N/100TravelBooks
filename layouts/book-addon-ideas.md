3. Additional Ideas to Enrich the Whole Book

A map section: A world map at the front or back showing approximate locations for all 100 books.

Regional clusters: Organize into themes or continents, or at least provide an index by region.

Timeline of publication: Helps show how travel writing evolved from the 19th century to now.

Mini-profiles of major writers: A few one-page interludes on giants like Freya Stark, Patrick Leigh Fermor, Kapuściński, Theroux.

Short essays:

“What makes a travel classic?”

“How travel writing changed after aviation”

“Women travelers who rewrote the genre”

“The rise of adventure and nature writing”
