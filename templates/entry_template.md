# Book Entry Template

Use this template for all 100 book entries to maintain consistency.

## Standard Format

```markdown
---

# **# [Entry Number] — [BOOK TITLE]**

### *[Subtitle if applicable]*

**"[Memorable opening quote from the book]"** — *[Author Name]*

---

### **[ The Essentials ]**

* **Genre:** [Primary genre - e.g., Literary Travelogue, Travel Memoir, etc.]
* **Sub-Genre:** [2-3 sub-genres - e.g., Quest Narrative, Cultural History, Survival]
* **Keywords:** *[5-7 evocative keywords in italics - e.g., Nomadic, Desolate, Resilient]*
* **Traveler Profile:** **[The archetype].** [1-2 sentences describing ideal reader - e.g., "The Intellectual Explorer. Best for the traveler who views every landscape as a library of stories."]

---

### **[ The Author: [Name] ([Birth Year]–[Death Year or "Present"]) ]**

[1-2 paragraphs about the author]
- Brief biographical background
- Why they're significant to travel writing
- Where this book fits in their body of work
- What drove them to this particular journey

### **[ The Book: *[Title]* ]**

[1-2 paragraphs about the book itself]
- What triggered or inspired this journey
- The narrative structure/approach
- Key themes or what makes it unique
- What the writer was seeking
- Major discoveries (geographical, cultural, personal)
- Literary significance or innovation

---

### **[ Destinations Explored ]**

* **[Region/Place 1]:** [Brief description - 1 line]
* **[Region/Place 2]:** [Brief description - 1 line]
* **[Region/Place 3]:** [Brief description - 1 line]
* **[Region/Place 4]:** [Brief description - 1 line]

[List 3-5 major destinations covered in the journey]

---

### **[ Further Reading ]**

**By [Author Name]:**

* ***[Other Book Title]***: [One sentence on why to read this]
* ***[Another Book Title]***: [One sentence on why to read this]

**In a Similar Vein:**

* ***[Book Title]*** by [Author]: [Why this pairs well - 1 sentence]
* ***[Book Title]*** by [Author]: [Why this pairs well - 1 sentence]

[Include 2-4 recommendations total - can be from within the 100 or outside]

---

### **[ The Signature Line ]**

> "[A defining quote from the book that captures its essence]"

### **[ The Perfect Pairing ]**

* **The Drink:** [What beverage fits the mood/setting]
* **The Soundtrack:** [What music or sounds accompany this journey]
* **The Setting:** [Where/when to read this book for maximum impact]

---
```

## Section Guidelines

### Entry Number
- Assign once final ordering is determined
- Can be chronological, thematic, or quality-based ranking

### Opening Quote
- Should be memorable and capture the book's voice
- Keep it short (1 sentence preferred, max 2)
- Can be from the author about travel, or a review quote about the book

### The Essentials
**Genre:** Choose primary from:
- Literary Travelogue
- Travel Memoir
- Adventure Narrative
- Journalistic Reportage
- Philosophical Travel
- Survival Story
- Spiritual Journey
- Expat Memoir
- Culinary Travel
- Nature Writing
- War/Conflict Reportage

**Sub-Genre:** 2-3 additional descriptors like:
- Quest Narrative, Cultural History, Cubist Non-Fiction, Self-Discovery, Political Commentary, Humor, Historical, Romantic, etc.

**Keywords:** 5-7 evocative adjectives/descriptors:
- Think: tone, mood, style, landscape
- Examples: Nomadic, Elliptical, Mythic, Desolate, Fragments, Resilient, Raw, Philosophical, Humorous, Lyrical

**Traveler Profile:** Create an archetype:
- The Intellectual Explorer
- The Soul-Searcher
- The Armchair Adventurer
- The Cultural Anthropologist
- The Spiritual Seeker
- The Reluctant Tourist
- The Gonzo Traveler
- The Historical Pilgrim
- Follow with 1-2 sentences on who will love this book

### The Author
**What to include:**
- Birth/death years
- Primary occupation besides writing (if applicable)
- What drove them to travel/write
- Their reputation in travel writing
- Other notable works (briefly)
- Any interesting biographical details that illuminate the book

**What to avoid:**
- Long career summaries
- Excessive personal details
- Information not relevant to this specific book

**Length:** 100-150 words

### The Book
**What to include:**
- Publication year (if not in title)
- When the journey took place (if different from publication)
- What sparked this particular journey
- The route/scope of travel
- Narrative structure or stylistic approach
- Central themes
- Why it's considered significant
- What makes it unique in travel literature

**What to avoid:**
- Detailed plot summary
- Spoilers of key revelations
- Excessive critical theory

**Length:** 150-200 words

### Destinations Explored
- List 3-5 major places/regions covered
- Each gets 1-line description (evocative, not encyclopedic)
- Use specific place names where possible
- Capture the essence of each location as portrayed in the book

Examples:
- "**The Mojave Desert:** The scorching, baptism-by-fire start of the trek."
- "**Buenos Aires:** The gateway to the southern wild."

### Further Reading
**By [Author]:**
- List 1-2 other works by same author (if they exist)
- Brief explanation of why to read each

**In a Similar Vein:**
- 1-2 books that pair well thematically, stylistically, or geographically
- Can reference other books in the 100 or outside recommendations
- One sentence explaining the connection

**Total:** 2-4 recommendations

### The Signature Line
- A quote that defines the book's philosophy or captures its essence
- Preferably about travel, place, or transformation
- Keep it powerful and concise
- Block quote format with >

### The Perfect Pairing
This adds personality and atmosphere. Be creative and specific:

**The Drink:**
- What fits the setting? (maté in Patagonia, purified stream water on the PCT, bitter tea in the Himalayas)
- Can be alcoholic, non-alcoholic, or metaphorical

**The Soundtrack:**
- Music, natural sounds, or silence
- What mood/sound accompanies this journey?

**The Setting:**
- Where should readers consume this book?
- Think: mood, season, environment
- Examples: "A tent zipper opening to a mountain sunrise" / "A remote cabin with a wood-burning stove during a storm"

## Length Targets

**Total word count per entry:** 300-500 words (excluding quotes, lists, section headers)

**Breakdown:**
- The Author: 100-150 words
- The Book: 150-200 words
- Other sections: Bullet points and short phrases

## Tone & Style

- **Encyclopedic but engaging**: Informative without being academic
- **Objective but opinionated**: Not personal memoir, but clear point of view on why book matters
- **Accessible**: Assume intelligent reader, but don't require PhD in lit theory
- **Evocative**: Use sensory details and strong verbs
- **Concise**: Every word earns its place

## Quality Checklist

Before marking an entry complete, verify:

- [ ] All sections present and properly formatted
- [ ] Author bio is accurate and relevant
- [ ] Book description focuses on significance, not just plot
- [ ] Keywords are evocative and accurate
- [ ] Traveler Profile clearly identifies target reader
- [ ] Destinations are specific and atmospheric
- [ ] Further Reading includes at least 2 recommendations
- [ ] Signature quote is powerful and representative
- [ ] Perfect Pairing is creative and fitting
- [ ] Total length is 300-500 words (excluding lists/quotes)
- [ ] Tone matches existing entries
- [ ] No spelling/grammar errors
- [ ] All facts checked (dates, place names, etc.)

## Research Sources

When writing entries for unread books:
- Publisher descriptions (but don't copy)
- Professional reviews (NYT, Guardian, literary journals)
- Author interviews
- Academic sources for historical context
- Goodreads/Amazon for reader perspective (sparingly)

**Always verify:**
- Publication year
- Journey dates (if different)
- Geographic accuracy
- Author biographical details
- Quotes (never fabricate)
